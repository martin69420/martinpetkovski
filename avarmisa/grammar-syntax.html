Avarmiša Grammar - Syntax

Basics

Avarmiša has a sentence order of SOV.

Noun Phrases

The order of components in a noun phrase goes like this:

Nouns in genitive case, possessives
Adverbs modifying adjectival verbs
Adjectival verbs, (in relative clause)
Adjectival verbs with subject and object, (in relative clause)
Other relative clauses
Noun adjuncts
Prepositional phrase
Phrase head

Example

Bajai uru saistisyža gonoin činja sapaija dypolamčaroboža saduiroin rum.
bad red DEF-bird-LOC rock.ACC throw DEF-person DIST-house-LOC DEF-apple-ACC eat
The bad person who threw a rock at the red bird is at that house over there, eating an apple.

Questions

The question marker, ju, is placed at the beginning of a question clause. However, in casual speech this marker may be omitted. Any interrogative can be placed in the space that would normally be occupied by a subject or object.

Example

Hamocasyšahlun, čaroža korušas.
hungry-GER-ABL home-LOC go-PPFV
I went home because I was hungry.

Ju bahanhlun čaroža korušas?
Q why-ABL home-LOC go-PPFV
Why did you go home?

Negatives

Negatives are formed by placing the word ny; 'no' before the verb as a prefix.

Example

Balsaža nykoruško.
shop-LOC NEG-go-FUT
I will not go to the store.

Relative Clauses

Relative clauses are formed with the relative clause preceding the head noun.

Example

Šomin rumlhurun damodz šoprotocyža korušhlurum.
shop-LOC go-FUT
The man who eats meat regularly goes to the butcher.

Time and place clauses

Time clauses are ended with the ablative suffix, -hlun, and place clauses are ended with the locative suffix, -ža.

Example

Hajlosancamohlun, erenjataža gimgimylas.
one_before_this_one-day-ABL stage-LOC dance-PIMPV
On the day before yesterday, I was dancing on a stage.

Conditionals

Conditionals are formed by placing the dependent clause, with a verb in the conditional mood, before the main clause, with a verb in future tense. This construction can be used for deductions or counterfactuals.

Example

Vasaraža nyčirikykorušas, ju čam kokoruš?
outside-LOC NEG-cold-COND Q you go-FUT.PFV
If it's not cold outside, will you go?

Causatives

Causatives are formed by placing an auxillary verb after the main verb. The auxillary verb to be used depends on the manner that the causer causes the object to perform an action.

Causer advises object: uracoio
 → Ly unin barbacalin komijoruracoias "I advised him to carry a weapon"
Causer forces object: jahunim
 → Ly unin haplanujahunimas "I forced him to jump"
Causer accidentally causes object: birajat
 → Ly unin dihinbirajatas "I accidentally made him write"
Causer intentionally causes object: (y)labijat
 → Ly unin dihinylabijatas "I made him write on purpose"
Causer naturally causes object: bokatok
 → Ly unin dihinbokatokas "I naturally made him write"
Causer causes object with effort: ganaridz
 → Ly unin dihinganaridzas "I got him to write"

Subordinate clauses

Subordinate conjunctions are placed after the gerund of the verb of the subordinate clause, followed by the main clause.

Examples

In the examples below, the subordinate conjunction will be in bold.

Lym jajukaruin rumša bakhloro, patalanoin rumšunkin.
1PL.IN sweetsACC eat-GER before vegetableACC eat-NEC
Before we eat our sweets, we should eat our vegetables.
